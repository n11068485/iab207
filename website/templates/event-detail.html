An Acknowledgement of Country is a statement that recognises the ongoing connection Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples have to their traditional lands, waters and communities. It is delivered at the start of a gathering as a genuine act of respect and a step toward reconciliation.

It is distinct from a Welcome to Country, which is a formal ceremony that can only be performed by an authorised Traditional Custodian of the specific land. An Acknowledgement can be made by anyone — Indigenous or non-Indigenous.

Australia's First Nations peoples hold the longest continuing cultures in human history. Acknowledging Country at public events recognises this history and supports the ongoing work of reconciliation.
